Verbi — quick reference

The 3-way decision — pick the stem by the TRIGGER WORD

Same verb, three different shrunk stems depending on what introduces the clause. Read the trigger, not the meaning you're aiming for — the trigger tells you everything.

The contracted-stem family (G-017) — futuro / condizionale only

These verbs don't use the full infinitive as their stem — the stem shrinks, then takes the normal short endings (-ò -ai -à -emo -ete -anno / -ei -esti -ebbe …). dovere is today's miss — it follows the exact same pattern as andare and venire, already solid.

Congiuntivo presente — beyond avere

Triggered by penso/credo/spero/sono contento che… ([[G-002]]) — the mistake that keeps recurring is memorizing ONE conjugated answer (e.g. siano) and reusing it regardless of person or verb. The trigger is what matters; the ending still has to match the actual subject.
Personessere (si-)stare (sti-)venire (veng-/ven-)

lorosianostianovengano
Regular verbs — the ending pattern (no memorizing needed, it's mechanical): -are verbs (parlare) → -i -i -i -iamo -iate -ino (io/tu/lui parli, loro parlino). -ere/-ire verbs (leggere, aprire, trovare-family aside) → -a -a -a -iamo -iate -ano (io/tu/lui legga, noi leggiamo, loro leggano; apra, troviate). Real slip on record: spero che tu ___ (trovare) → wrote trovate (indicative) instead of troviate (congiuntivo) — one letter, -a- vs -i-.

Congiuntivo imperfetto — so far only avere

Triggered by a real se ("if"): Se avessi tempo, verrei. Full avere paradigm is in the first card above (avess- stem). No other verb has been taught in this mood yet — don't extrapolate a form you haven't seen confirmed.

Imperfetto — hidden-stem verbs

A few verbs keep an old, longer stem in the imperfetto instead of the expected infinitive-minus-ending — then take the normal -vo -vi -va -vamo -vate -vano tail. essere is separately, fully irregular (no -vo tail at all).

Reflexive present tense

The clitic (mi/ti/si/ci/vi/si) sits BEFORE a conjugated verb, or welds onto the back of an infinitive ([[S-006]], devo alzarmi). The verb ending itself is completely normal for its class — only the front pronoun is "reflexive."
